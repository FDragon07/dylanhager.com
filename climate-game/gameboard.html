Rules

Players: 6-8
Time: 60-120 minutes

How to Win

  • If the global temperature is less than 1.5°C by 2050, everyone wins.
  • If the global temperature is between 1.5°C and 2°C, only countries that have completed their secondary objectives win.
  • If the global temperature rises above 2°C, the game ends, and everyone loses.

Goals

  • Reduce your carbon emissions by investing in renewable energy.
  • Reduce fossil fuel production to lower global CO₂ emissions.
  • Invest in education to make it easier to fund renewable energy initiatives.
  • Set aside money in an emergency climate fund to respond to disasters.
  • Contribute to the global renewable energy fund to support underdeveloped countries.

Setup

Assign each player a country, money per round indicator, fossil fuel production indicators, and a secondary mission. Each player should also receive a D-100 (two D-10 dice).

Gameplay

The game starts in 2020 and ends in 2050. Each round represents 2 years. Players receive money at the start of each round, which they can allocate to various actions.

Actions Per Turn

  • Invest in renewable energy to reduce emissions.
  • Reduce or increase fossil fuel production, affecting emissions and income.
  • Invest in or defund education, impacting policy success rates.
  • Set aside money in an emergency fund for disasters.
  • Contribute to the global renewable energy fund.

Disasters

At the end of each round, draw disaster cards. Countries must use their emergency funds to mitigate disasters. If insufficient funds are available, the cost is doubled and deducted from future income.

How to Vote on Policies

To pass a policy, roll a D-100. A roll of 51 or higher is required. For every 5% increase in funding, roll an additional time and take the lowest result.

Education's Role

Investing in education reduces the number of rolls required to pass policies. Defunding education increases the number of rolls required.
